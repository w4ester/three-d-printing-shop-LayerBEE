Work With Your Parents!

Running a business - even a small one - involves money, legal stuff, and talking to strangers online. Always:

  • Get parent/guardian approval before selling anything
  • Never share personal info (address, phone, school)
  • Have an adult handle payments and shipping
  • Meet customers in public places with an adult present

Step 1: Calculate Material Cost

Filament Math:
  • 1 kg spool = 1000 grams
  • $20 spool Ã· 1000g = $0.02 per gram
  • Your slicer tells you gram weight!
  • Example: 50g print Ã— $0.02 = $1.00 in filament

Step 2: Add Your Time

1

Set Your Hourly Rate

Start with $5-15/hour depending on your skill level. You're worth it!

2

Count ALL Your Time

Setup, slicing, monitoring, post-processing, packaging. Not just print time!

3

Active vs Passive Time

Print time runs while you do other stuff. Count it at 10-20% of your rate.

Step 3: Add Overhead (The Hidden Costs)

Cost Type What It Covers Rule of Thumb
Electricity Power for printer $0.10-0.20/hour
Wear & Tear Nozzles, beds, parts 5-10% of material
Failed Prints Not every print works 10-15% buffer

Photo Checklist

1

Natural Light is Best

Shoot near a window during daytime. Avoid harsh direct sunlight - cloudy days are perfect!

2

Clean Background

White, gray, or solid color. Nothing distracting. Poster board works great!

3

Multiple Angles

Front, side, top, detail shots. Show the whole product and close-ups.

4

Show Scale

Include a coin, ruler, or common object so buyers know the size.

5

Lifestyle Shots

Show the product in use! A phone stand with a phone, a planter with a plant.

Bad Photo

Dark, messy background, blurry, harsh shadows, no scale

Good Photo

Bright, clean background, sharp focus, soft shadows, shows size

Great Photo

All the above + lifestyle context + multiple angles

Free Editing: Use your phone's built-in editor to:
  • Increase brightness slightly
  • Crop to remove distractions
  • Straighten if needed
  • Don't over-filter - buyers want to see the real product!

Lesson 5: Customer Service

Happy customers = repeat customers + referrals. Treat them well!

Communication Tips

1

Respond Quickly

Answer questions within 24 hours. Set expectations if you're busy with school.

2

Be Professional

Use proper spelling and grammar. Be friendly but businesslike.

3

Set Clear Expectations

Tell customers exactly what they're getting and when they'll get it.

4

Handle Problems Gracefully

If something goes wrong, apologize and fix it. Offer refunds or replacements when needed.

What to Include With Orders

Packaging Touches:
  • Thank you note (handwritten is best!)
  • Care instructions if needed
  • Business card or flyer for reorders
  • Protective packaging so it arrives safe
  • A small extra (sticker, small print) delights customers!
