Quick Fixes (Try These First!)

1

Clean Your Bed

Wipe with isopropyl alcohol (90%+). Oils from your fingers can prevent adhesion. This fixes 50% of adhesion problems!

2

Level the Bed

The nozzle might be too far from the bed. Re-level or use Z-offset to get closer. Paper should just drag when leveling.

3

Increase Bed Temperature

Try 5-10Â°C higher. PLA: 60-65Â°C. PETG: 75-85Â°C.

4

Add a Brim

In your slicer, add a brim (5-10mm). This gives more surface area to stick.

Still Not Working?

Adhesion Helpers:
  • Glue stick - Apply thin layer, let dry. Works great on glass beds!
  • Hairspray - Light coat of unscented hairspray
  • Painter's tape - Blue tape works well for PLA
  • PEI sheet - Upgrade your bed surface for best adhesion

The Fix: Retraction Settings

Retraction pulls filament back when the nozzle travels, preventing ooze.

Setting Bowden Tube Direct Drive Notes
Distance 4-8mm 0.5-2mm How far to pull back
Speed 40-60mm/s 25-40mm/s How fast to retract

Other Stringing Fixes

1

Lower Temperature

Try 5-10Â°C lower. Hotter plastic is more runny and oozes more.

2

Increase Travel Speed

Faster moves = less time for oozing. Try 150-200mm/s for travel moves.

3

Dry Your Filament

Wet filament causes extra oozing. Dry it in a food dehydrator or filament dryer.

4

Enable "Combing"

This setting keeps travel moves inside the print, hiding any strings.

Quick Remove: Minor stringing can be removed with a heat gun (briefly!) or by carefully picking off with tweezers.

Prevention is Key

1

Use a Heated Bed

Keep the bed warm to slow cooling. PLA: 60Â°C, PETG: 80Â°C, ABS: 100-110Â°C

2

Eliminate Drafts

Keep windows closed and AC vents away. Cold air causes uneven cooling.

3

Use a Brim or Raft

Brims add grip at edges. Rafts provide a stable base for the whole print.

4

Add Mouse Ears

Small discs at corners (in slicer or design) add extra adhesion where needed.

ABS and ASA: These materials warp the most! Use an enclosure (even a cardboard box) to keep heat in. Consider switching to PETG for less warping.

Problem: Layer Issues

Layer Shifting

When layers suddenly shift to one side, creating a "staircase" effect.

Loose Belts

Belts should twang like a guitar string. Tighten if floppy.

Printing Too Fast

Slow down! Stepper motors can skip steps at high speeds.

Loose Pulleys

Check that set screws on pulleys are tight.

Layer Gaps / Under-Extrusion

Gaps in layers or walls that look thin and weak.

1

Check Filament Path

Make sure filament feeds smoothly. No tangles on the spool!

2

Increase Flow Rate

Try 105-110% flow rate in your slicer to push more plastic.

3

Check Nozzle

Partial clogs cause under-extrusion. Do a cold pull to clean.

4

Increase Temperature

Filament might not be melting fast enough. Try 5-10Â°C higher.

Z-Banding / Inconsistent Layers

Horizontal lines or waves visible on the print surface.

Common Causes:
  • Z-rod needs lubrication (use PTFE-based lube)
  • Z-rod is bent (shine flashlight behind it to check)
  • Inconsistent bed temperature (PID tune your bed)
  • Loose Z-axis wheels or lead screw nut

Problem: Clogged Nozzle

Nothing coming out? Partial extrusion? Here's how to fix it.

Signs of a Clog

No Extrusion

Extruder clicking but nothing coming out

Inconsistent Flow

Filament comes out in spurts or thin

Grinding

Extruder gear eating into filament

The Cold Pull Method

1

Heat to Print Temp

Heat nozzle to your normal printing temperature.

2

Push Filament Through

Manually push filament through to get flow started.

3

Cool to 90Â°C (PLA) or 160Â°C (PETG)

Let nozzle cool with filament still in it.

4

Pull Firmly

Yank the filament out. The tip should have debris stuck to it!

5

Repeat if Needed

Do 2-3 cold pulls until filament tip comes out clean.

When to Replace the Nozzle: Nozzles wear out over time, especially when printing abrasive filaments (glow-in-dark, carbon fiber). If cold pulls don't help, swap in a new nozzle. They're cheap!

Problem: Poor Surface Quality

Blobs and Zits

Small bumps on the surface, especially at layer start points.

1

Enable "Coasting"

Stops extrusion slightly before the end of each line to reduce pressure.

2

Adjust Retraction

Too little = blobs. Too much = gaps. Find the sweet spot.

3

Lower Temperature

Hotter plastic oozes more. Try reducing by 5Â°C.

4

Hide the Seam

Set "Z seam alignment" to "Sharpest corner" to hide layer starts.

Rough Overhangs

Droopy or rough surfaces on angled parts.

Overhang Solutions:
  • Add supports for angles over 45Â°
  • Lower temperature for better bridging
  • Increase cooling fan to 100%
  • Slow down print speed for overhangs
  • Reorient model to reduce overhangs

Elephant's Foot

First layer is wider than the rest, creating a "foot" at the bottom.

Cause

Nozzle too close, bed too hot, or too much first layer squish

Fix

Raise Z-offset slightly, lower bed temp 5Â°C, or use "elephant foot compensation" in slicer

Post-Fix

Sand the bottom edge with 220 grit sandpaper
