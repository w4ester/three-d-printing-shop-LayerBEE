What Happens at Each Step?

1

Design (STL File)

Create or download a 3D model. STL files describe the shape but don't tell the printer HOW to make it.

2

Slice (G-code File)

The slicer "cuts" your model into thin layers and creates instructions (G-code) telling the printer exactly where to move and how much filament to push out.

3

Transfer

Get the G-code file to your printer via SD card, USB drive, or network connection.

4

Print!

Your printer reads the G-code and builds your object layer by layer.

Pro Tip: Most print problems come from Step 2 (slicing). Getting your slicer settings right is the key to great prints!

Option B: Design Your Own in Tinkercad

Tinkercad is a free, browser-based CAD tool perfect for beginners. No downloads needed!

1

Create an Account

Go to tinkercad.com and sign up (free). You can use a school account or create one with parent help.

2

Start a New Design

Click "Create new design" to open the 3D workspace.

3

Drag and Drop Shapes

Use basic shapes (boxes, cylinders, spheres) from the right panel. Combine them to make complex objects!

4

Export as STL

Click "Export" in the top right and choose ".STL" to download your model.

The Big Five Settings

1

Layer Height

0.2mm is the sweet spot. Lower = smoother but slower. Higher = faster but rougher.

2

Infill Percentage

15-20% for most prints. Higher for strong parts, 0% for vases (with vase mode on).

3

Print Speed

50mm/s for quality, up to 100mm/s for fast prints. New printers like Bambu can go much faster!

4

Supports

Turn ON for overhangs over 45Â°. Use "tree supports" for easier removal.

5

Bed Adhesion

Brim adds a flat ring around your print for better stick. Raft adds layers underneath (slower but very stable).

Getting the File to Your Printer

SD Card

Most reliable. Works with all printers. Just copy and eject!

USB Drive

Same as SD card. Make sure printer has USB port.

WiFi/Network

Send directly from slicer. Convenient but needs setup.

Watching the First Layer

The first layer is the most critical. Stay and watch it!

Good First Layer

Smooth, slightly squished lines. No gaps. Sticks firmly.

Too High

Round spaghetti lines. Not sticking. Gaps between lines.

Too Low

Thin, transparent. Scraping sounds. Hard to remove.

First Layer Fix: Most slicers have a "Z offset" or "baby stepping" option. If your first layer looks wrong, adjust it during the print! Lower the nozzle if too high, raise if too low.
