What is 3D Printing?

ğŸ Fun Fact: 3D printing was invented in 1984 - that's probably before your parents were born!

3D printing (also called additive manufacturing) builds objects layer by layer from the bottom up. It's like building with really thin Lego bricks, but the "bricks" are made of melted plastic!

FDM Printing

FDM stands for Fused Deposition Modeling. Don't worry about the fancy name - here's what it means:

  • Fused = Melted together
  • Deposition = Putting down material
  • Modeling = Making a shape

So FDM just means "melting plastic and putting it down in layers to make a shape." Simple!

Getting Your First Print Right

The secret to good prints? A perfect first layer!

1

Level Your Bed

The nozzle should be the same distance from the bed everywhere. Use the "paper test" - a piece of paper should have slight drag when pulled between the nozzle and bed.

2

Clean the Bed

Use isopropyl alcohol (IPA) to remove oils and dust. A clean bed = better adhesion!

3

Heat It Up

For PLA: Nozzle ~200Â°C, Bed ~60Â°C. Let it heat fully before starting.

4

Watch the First Layer

Stay and watch! The first layer should be slightly squished into the bed - not too high, not too squished.

Lines don't stick, spaghetti mess

âœ… Just Right

âŒ Too Low
