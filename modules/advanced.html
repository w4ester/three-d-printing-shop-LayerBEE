Method 3: Paint It!

Sometimes the easiest multi-color method is post-print painting. Print in one color, paint the details.

Best Paints for 3D Prints:
  • Acrylic paints - Cheap, easy to use, water cleanup
  • Spray paint + primer - Great for base coats
  • Alcohol markers - Good for small details
  • Washes - Thin paint that settles in crevices for weathering effects

Safe Speed Increases

1

Infill Speed: Go Fast!

Infill is hidden inside. Crank it up to 80-150mm/s. Nobody will see it!

2

Travel Speed: Maximum

Travel moves don't extrude, so go as fast as your printer allows (150-300mm/s).

3

Inner Walls: Moderate

Inner walls are covered by outer walls. 60-80mm/s is usually fine.

4

Outer Walls: Keep Slower

These are what you see. Keep at 40-60mm/s for best quality.

Speed Limits: Your printer has physical limits. Going too fast causes:
  • Layer shifts (motors skip steps)
  • Ringing/ghosting (vibration artifacts)
  • Under-extrusion (can't melt plastic fast enough)

Input Shaper (Klipper Users)

If your printer runs Klipper firmware, Input Shaper can reduce ringing and allow much higher speeds!

Klipper Speed Benefits:
  • Run accelerometer calibration to measure resonance
  • Klipper compensates for vibrations automatically
  • Print 2-3x faster with same quality!
  • Works on many printers with firmware upgrade

Lesson 3: Post-Processing

Turn good prints into great ones with finishing techniques!

Sanding

Wet Sanding

Use water to reduce dust. Start 220 grit â†’ 400 â†’ 800 â†’ 1000+

Dry Sanding

Faster but dusty. Wear a mask! Good for rough shaping.

Skip Grits

Don't jump from 220 to 1000. You'll see scratches!

Filling & Priming

1

Fill Layer Lines

Use filler primer spray or wood filler to fill gaps. Sand smooth when dry.

2

Spot Putty

For bigger gaps, use automotive spot putty. Apply thin, sand when cured.

3

Primer Coat

Gray primer reveals imperfections. Fix them, then final prime.

Vapor Smoothing (ABS/ASA Only)

Safety First: Acetone vapor smoothing uses dangerous fumes!
  • Only for ABS or ASA plastic (not PLA!)
  • Do outdoors or with very good ventilation
  • Keep away from flames - acetone is flammable
  • Have an adult supervise

Vase Mode Tips:
  • Enable in slicer: "Spiralize Outer Contour" or "Vase Mode"
  • Use 0.6mm nozzle for stronger walls
  • Great for vases, lampshades, bowls, pencil cups
  • Can't have multiple shells or infill

Ironing

Get perfectly smooth top surfaces by "ironing" them with the hot nozzle.

How to Enable:
  • In Cura: Search "ironing" and enable
  • In PrusaSlicer: Print Settings â†’ Infill â†’ Enable ironing
  • Adds print time but gives glass-smooth tops
  • Best for flat-topped objects like boxes or signs

Fuzzy Skin

Add a textured, rough surface for grip or organic looks!

Fuzzy Skin Settings:
  • Enable in slicer under "Experimental" or "Surface" settings
  • Adjust "Fuzzy Skin Thickness" (0.1-0.5mm)
  • Great for grips, organic models, hiding layer lines
  • Can't be sanded smooth - choose wisely!
